History

Introduction

Rho Tau Gamma (RTG) Fraternity was founded on December 4, 1960, at Luzonian Colleges, now known as Manuel S. Enverga University Foundation (MSEUF), in the Philippines. Established by a group of students committed to intellectual growth and principled leadership, the fraternity was formed as an honor-oriented brotherhood guided by ideals of right thinking, truth, and faith.

Historical Background

During the early 1960s, student organizations played a significant role in campus life at Luzonian Colleges. A group of students envisioned forming a new fraternity that would promote academic excellence, ethical leadership, and constructive competition within the university community. This vision laid the foundation for what would become Rho Tau Gamma.

The Founding – December 4, 1960

The first formal meeting of the fraternity took place on December 4, 1960, at the Antigua Restaurant. Approximately twenty students attended the gathering to discuss the establishment of a new organization. Among the founding members were Ruperto Zarsuelo, Roman Mendioro, Rolando Ramos, Eutiquio Telada, Joseph Tan Palanca, Anselmo Regis, Conrado Sio, and Esmeraldo Palomar, then editor-in-chief of The Luzonian, the college publication.

Origin of the Name

The name “Rho Tau Gamma” was adopted to symbolize the fraternity’s guiding philosophy. Interpreted as “Right Thinkers Group,” the name reflects the founders’ emphasis on intellectual discipline, moral responsibility, and faith. The fraternity’s principles are traditionally associated with the values of Right, Truth, and God.

Drafting of the Constitution

During the founding meeting, Roman Mendioro and Ruperto Zarsuelo presented a draft constitution to guide the structure and governance of the organization. Esmeraldo Palomar was tasked with preparing the written document, incorporating revisions and suggestions from the founding members. The constitution outlined the fraternity’s objectives, membership standards, and leadership structure.

Election of the First Officers

The initial officers elected during the founding period were:

  • Sir Lord Rhoan – Ruperto Zarsuelo
  • Lord Rhoan – Roman Mendioro
  • Rhoan Annalist – Esmeraldo Palomar
  • Rhoan Scroller – Conrado Sio
  • Keeper of the Chest – Joseph Tan Palanca
  • Guardian of the Chest – Rolando Ramos
  • Rhoan Transactor – Eutiquio Telada
  • Rhoan Knights – Efren Mercado and Francisco Serrano

Symbols and Identity

The fraternity adopted the motto “Cogitare Est Vivere”, translated as “To Think Is To Live.” The organization also established its official emblem, fraternity pin, and formal attire to represent its identity and traditions.

Institutional Recognition

Following its organization, the founders sought formal recognition from the administration of Luzonian Colleges. Meetings were held with Dr. Tobias Y. Enverga, then Dean of Colleges, along with faculty members and administrators. After evaluation and review, the fraternity received official institutional recognition on September 7, 1962. Rho Tau Gamma became one of the fraternities granted a charter of recognition by the institution.

Growth and Development

In the years following its recognition, Rho Tau Gamma continued to participate in campus activities and student leadership initiatives. The fraternity maintained its commitment to academic standards, fellowship, and service within the university community.

Legacy

Since its founding in 1960, Rho Tau Gamma has remained part of the institutional history of Manuel S. Enverga University Foundation. Guided by its founding principles, the fraternity continues to uphold its traditions and organizational identity.
